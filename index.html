About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Université de Montréal, formerly a Ph.D. student at Paris Cité University under the supervision of Professor Régis De La Bretèche Régis De La Bretèche. Prior to that, I completed a Master of Science in Applied Mathematics with a specialization in Machine Learning and Engineering from École CentraleSupélec, as well as a Master of Science in Pure Mathematics from Université Paris Cité. My research centers on analytic and combinatorial number theory, with a particular focus on random multiplicative functions. I use probabilistic tools such as multiplicative chaos and harmonic analysis to investigate number-theoretic questions, especially those related to moments of character sums and Dirichlet polynomials.

Beyond number theory, I am also interested in the intersection of artificial intelligence and mathematics, including graph theory for AI, graph neural networks (GNNs), small language models, and reasoning-oriented model design.
